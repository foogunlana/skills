## Origin

> *Every trip starts with a destination and a mess of unknowns — dates,
> budget, airlines, stops. Trip Planner fills in the blanks so you
> don't have to. Assume first. Show your work. Book the flight.*

## Workflow Diagram

```
           ┌──────────────────┐
           │   DESCRIBE TRIP  │  "Flights to Tokyo next month"
           │   (10 seconds)   │  Natural language, any detail level
           └────────┬─────────┘
                    │
                    v
           ┌──────────────────┐
           │  ASSUME & SHOW   │  Fill every gap with smart defaults
           │   (instant)      │  Show assumption card for review
           └────────┬─────────┘
                    │
                    v
           ┌──────────────────┐
           │  SEARCH & RANK   │  WebSearch + booking URLs

           └────────┬─────────┘
                    │
                    v
           ┌──────────────────┐
           │  CORRECT & BOOK  │  Adjust anything, re-search
           │   (you decide)   │  Direct booking links provided
```

## Key Concepts

- One requirement: where you're going. Everything else is assumed.
- Every assumption is visible — you correct what's wrong, not fill in what's missing.
- Multi-city trips are first-class: "London → Paris → Rome → London" just works.
- Direct booking links for every recommendation.
- Preferences saved once, used every time.
